Learn ASL

Between 500,000 and 2 million people use American Sign Language (ASL) in the United States.

This makes ASL the fifth most-used language in the United States behind Spanish, Italian, German and French. It is important to understand that ASL is different from spoken English or other forms of sign language used in other geographical locations or regions. However, understanding ASL can only enhance communication skills between parties. For one, many early childhood experts believe that babies learn to speak through the use of signs before they are able to verbalize their needs. Furthermore, ASL allows us to communicate with more than 17% of the population in the U.S. alone as there are approximately 36 million hard of hearing and deaf people who reside there.
